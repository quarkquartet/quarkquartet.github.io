Welcome

I’m Isaac Wang, a Ph.D Candidate at Rutgers University. I work at the frontier of particle physics and cosmology.

I’m particularly interested in the origin of matter-antimatter asymmetry, dynamics of electroweak phase transitions, topological defects of cosmological phase transitions, axion rotations and gratitational waves. I also have some experience working on the more experimental-oriented side, proposing interesting probes in modern experiments. My publications can be found here.

Interests
  • Dynamics of Cosmological Phase Transitions
  • High Scale Baryogenesis, Phase Transitions and Gravitational Waves
  • Axion Rotations, Axion Kination and Axiogenesis
